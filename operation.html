Section 2 • Seed Planting

Planting Technique

When planting germinated seeds, two things must be correct every time: orientation and depth.

Root & Leaf Orientation

Leaves / shoot point UP
The pointed plumule (shoot) faces upward toward the surface.
Root / radicle points DOWN
The radicle (root) faces downward into the soil.

Wrong orientation slows growth or kills the seed. Always check before covering.

Planting Depth

soil surface
seed level
  • Plant at the recommended shallow depth — just covered.
  • Too deep delays emergence; too shallow exposes the seed.
  • Firm the soil gently and water in after planting.

Section 5

Beyond the Routine: Quality & Movement

On top of daily operations, three activities control quality and move seedlings toward sale.

Culling

Removing damaged or off-type seedlings at several checkpoints so only healthy ones continue.

Transplanting

Moving selected seedlings from pre-nursery trays into larger main-nursery bags.

Collection of Seedling

Selecting, loading and handing over seedlings to the customer at the point of sale.

Section 5 • Culling

Culling at Every Stage

Seed-Damage Culling

During seed planting

Damaged or non-viable seeds are removed as we plant.

1st Culling

Month 3–5 · before transplant

Picking is done before transplant. At the plot, workers only transplant.

2nd Culling

Month 4 · after transplant

Drone mapping identifies and counts dead palms in the main plot.

3rd Culling

At point of sale

Drone mapping is used again to count and cull when the plot is sold.
